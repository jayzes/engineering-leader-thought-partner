# Engineering Career Ladder

## Philosophy

This ladder describes **expectations for sustained performance** at each level, not checkboxes for promotion. Promotion means someone is already operating at the next level consistently.

## Levels Overview

| Level | Title | Scope | Experience (typical) |
|-------|-------|-------|---------------------|
| L1 | Software Engineer I | Task | 0-2 years |
| L2 | Software Engineer II | Feature | 2-4 years |
| L3 | Senior Software Engineer | Project | 4-7 years |
| L4 | Staff Software Engineer | Team/Multi-team | 7+ years |
| L5 | Senior Staff Engineer | Org/Company | 10+ years |
| L6 | Principal Engineer | Industry | 15+ years |

---

## L1: Software Engineer I

### Scope
Individual tasks within defined projects.

### Technical Skills
- Delivers well-defined tasks with guidance
- Writes clean, readable code
- Follows team conventions and standards
- Debugs issues in their own code
- Asks for help appropriately

### Collaboration
- Communicates progress and blockers clearly
- Receptive to feedback on code reviews
- Learning team processes and norms

### Impact
- Completes assigned work on time
- Growing independence over time

---

## L2: Software Engineer II

### Scope
Features and small projects within a team.

### Technical Skills
- Owns features from design to deployment
- Makes sound technical decisions within scope
- Writes testable, maintainable code
- Debugs across systems they work in
- Reviews code effectively

### Collaboration
- Communicates technical concepts clearly
- Helps onboard new team members
- Participates actively in team discussions

### Impact
- Consistently delivers quality features
- Improves team processes and documentation
- Reduces technical debt as they encounter it

---

## L3: Senior Software Engineer

### Scope
Projects and initiatives within a team.

### Technical Skills
- Leads technical design for complex features
- Makes sound architectural decisions
- Anticipates and mitigates technical risk
- Deep expertise in team's domain
- Debugs complex cross-system issues

### Collaboration
- Mentors junior engineers effectively
- Drives technical discussions
- Works well with product/design partners
- Clear written and verbal communication

### Impact
- Multiplies team effectiveness
- Drives significant improvements to systems
- Sets technical direction for team initiatives

---

## L4: Staff Software Engineer

### Scope
Multiple teams or significant technical domain.

### Technical Skills
- Leads large, ambiguous technical initiatives
- Makes tradeoffs across competing concerns
- Sets technical direction at team/multi-team level
- Deep expertise, broad awareness
- Debugs the hardest problems

### Collaboration
- Influences without formal authority
- Builds consensus across teams
- Effective communicator with executives
- Mentors senior engineers

### Impact
- Drives org-wide technical improvements
- Identifies and solves systemic problems
- Creates leverage for other engineers

### Archetypes
- **Tech Lead**: Guides team technical direction
- **Architect**: Drives cross-team technical vision
- **Solver**: Tackles the hardest problems
- **Right Hand**: Extends executive capacity

---

## L5: Senior Staff Engineer

### Scope
Organization or significant company function.

### Technical Skills
- Sets technical strategy for large areas
- Navigates extreme ambiguity
- Balances technical and business concerns
- Industry-relevant expertise
- Trusted for the most critical decisions

### Collaboration
- Influences company-wide technical culture
- Partners effectively with executives
- Develops other staff engineers
- Represents engineering externally

### Impact
- Shapes company technical direction
- Solves company-existential technical problems
- Creates significant competitive advantage

---

## L6: Principal Engineer

### Scope
Company-wide and/or industry influence.

### Technical Skills
- Defines technical vision at company level
- Anticipates industry trends
- Makes bet-the-company technical decisions
- Recognized expertise externally

### Collaboration
- Trusted advisor to executives
- Shapes engineering culture company-wide
- Develops senior staff engineers
- Represents company in industry

### Impact
- Creates lasting technical competitive advantage
- Influences industry direction
- Attracts top talent to the company

---

## Assessment Dimensions

When evaluating engineers, consider:

1. **Technical Skill**: Quality of technical work and decisions
2. **Scope**: Size and complexity of problems they own
3. **Collaboration**: How they work with others
4. **Leadership**: How they enable others' success
5. **Impact**: Results they produce

---

## Promotion Process

1. **Self-assessment**: Engineer documents their work against next level
2. **Manager assessment**: Manager gathers feedback and evaluates
3. **Calibration**: Managers calibrate across teams
4. **Decision**: Promotion committee reviews and decides
5. **Communication**: Results shared with engineer

### Promotion criteria
- Already operating at next level for 6+ months
- Peer feedback supports the assessment
- Organizational need exists at that level
